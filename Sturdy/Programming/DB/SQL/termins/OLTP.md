stands for **Online Transaction Processing**.

- It is designed to handle a large number of short, simple operations like inserting, updating, or deleting records.
- Common in systems that support day-to-day business operations.

#### **Key Features**:

- **Fast and frequent transactions**: For example, recording a bank deposit or an online order.
- **Real-time data**: Keeps data up-to-date for operational purposes.
- **Normalized databases**: Data is organized into smaller tables with relationships, reducing redundancy.
- **Examples**: ATM systems, e-commerce websites, CRM tools.

#### **Example**:

Imagine you're booking a flight:

1. You search for available flights (read operation).
2. You book a flight (write operation).
3. The system updates the seat availability (update operation).
